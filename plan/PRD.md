# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

# English Companion

**Version:** 1.0 (MVP)

**Status:** Draft - Product Foundation

**Owner:** Project Author

---

# 1. Executive Summary

English Companion is an AI-powered Progressive Web Application (PWA) designed to help learners build the habit of using English every day through natural conversations, writing, vocabulary practice, and personalized AI feedback.

Unlike traditional language learning platforms that focus on memorization, grammar drills, or gamification alone, English Companion focuses on one simple mission:

> **Helping users become comfortable communicating in English.**

The application is built around the idea that confidence comes from consistent usage rather than perfect grammar.

Instead of acting as a teacher who constantly evaluates mistakes, the AI behaves as a supportive English companion that encourages conversation while providing personalized coaching at appropriate moments.

The first MVP is intended primarily for personal use but is designed with a scalable architecture so it can evolve into a public platform in the future.

---

# 2. Vision

Build an AI companion that helps people naturally think, speak, and write in English through daily practice.

The application should become something users genuinely want to open every day—not because they have to, but because they enjoy the experience.

---

# 3. Mission

Help learners replace passive English study with active English usage.

The product should transform learning from:

"I studied English today."

into

"I used English today."

---

# 4. Problem Statement

Many learners spend years studying English but still hesitate to communicate.

Common problems include:

* Fear of making mistakes.
* Lack of confidence.
* No speaking partner.
* Vocabulary is remembered but rarely used.
* Grammar knowledge exists but is difficult to apply.
* Motivation decreases after several weeks.
* Learning feels like taking endless exams.

Most language learning applications optimize for lesson completion instead of communication.

English Companion aims to solve this problem.

# 16. Grammar Coaching

Grammar explanations should be adaptive.

First mistake

Small correction.

Repeated mistake

Brief explanation.

Repeated multiple times

Detailed explanation with examples.

Persistent weakness

Recommend reviewing the related lesson.

The AI should never overload users with grammar.

---

# 17. Grammar Notes

After conversations users receive Grammar Notes.

Each note contains

* Original sentence
* Improved sentence
* Why it changed
* Grammar rule
* Pattern
* Examples
* Small practice challenge

Example

Original

I go yesterday.

Improved

I went yesterday.

Why?

Yesterday indicates a finished action.

Grammar

Simple Past

Pattern

Subject + Verb 2

Examples

I visited my friend.

She cooked dinner.

Practice

Try writing another sentence using "went."

# 27. Risks

Potential risks include

* AI responses becoming repetitive.
* Over-correcting users.
* Long conversations consuming excessive AI tokens.
* Users losing motivation.
* Recommendations becoming irrelevant.

These risks should be mitigated through adaptive learning, conversation memory, concise prompting, and a coaching-first AI philosophy.

---

# 28. Product Principles

Before implementing any feature, always ask:

1. Does this help users use English more often?
2. Does this increase confidence?
3. Does this encourage consistency?
4. Does this keep the experience enjoyable?
5. Can this feature remain simple?

If the answer to most questions is "no," the feature does not belong in the MVP.

# 29. Product Vision Statement

English Companion exists to help people stop worrying about perfect English and start living with English.

The application is successful when users no longer think of English as a subject to study, but as a language they naturally use every day.

The ultimate goal is not perfect grammar.

The ultimate goal is building confidence through consistent communication.
